Background

The book, the method, and the practice behind both

Cory Maki is the author of Reddit, AI Overviews & GEO: The SaaS Founder's Playbook for Winning AI Search Visibility and the creator of the ARC Method — a 90-day framework for earning citations in AI-generated answers from Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot. The book was released April 6, 2026, and the method is its operational spine. Together, they are how more than a decade of search and reputation work gets distilled into something a SaaS founder can actually run, in sequence, against a calendar.

Maki is based in Taichung, Taiwan. The discipline he writes about — Generative Engine Optimization, or GEO — is the natural extension of a long career in SEO and online reputation management into a search layer that did not exist five years ago. The fundamentals of how authority gets built online have not gone away. The systems that surface and synthesize that authority have, and the book is an argument about what to do about it.

The ARC Method

Audit, Reddit & Reputation, Citability — in 90 days

The ARC Method is Cory's proprietary framework for earning citations in AI-generated answers, and it is the centerpiece of Reddit, AI Overviews & GEO. It runs in three phases over a 90-day calendar. The Audit phase measures current AI visibility across the five major platforms and identifies the citation gaps where competitors appear and the client does not. The Reddit and Reputation phase builds off-site authority on the platforms AI models trust most — and the data on Reddit's outsized role in AI citations is the part that surprises most founders. Reddit accounts for roughly 21% of all Google AI Overview citations and around 46.5% of Perplexity's citations, which makes it the single highest-leverage off-site channel for AI visibility.

The Citability phase then restructures on-site content so AI systems can extract, cite, and recommend it accurately, with attention to passage-level structure, schema markup, and cross-source factual consistency. The full implementation sequence — week by week, with templates, scoring criteria, and platform-specific quick references — is laid out in the book. The 90-day timeline is deliberate: the work is slower than buying ads and faster than waiting for organic SEO to compound, and it rewards the kind of sustained presence that founders who treat Reddit as a campaign channel rarely get back.

What He Believes

Six positions that shape the book

Cory's voice in Reddit, AI Overviews & GEO is authoritative but practical, evidence-led, and consistently founder-friendly. He stakes out a middle position on the most contested question in the field: GEO is an evolution, not a revolution. SEO fundamentals remain essential; GEO adds a specific layer on top. He is openly skeptical of GEO tool vendors selling dashboards before the discipline is mature enough to know what those dashboards should measure — a position he develops in his commentary on the GEO-versus-SEO debate.

The other beliefs that shape the book: Reddit is the highest-leverage channel for AI visibility, especially for SaaS; each AI platform has distinct citation patterns and a unified "AI SEO" approach is too coarse to be useful; AI extracts passages, not pages, so content structure matters at the sentence level; and cross-source factual consistency — your bio, your founding date, your credentials being identical across every property you control — is a real and underappreciated ranking factor in AI synthesis.
